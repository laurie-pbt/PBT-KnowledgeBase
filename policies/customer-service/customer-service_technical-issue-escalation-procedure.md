# Technical Issue Escalation Procedure

## Technical Issue Escalation Procedure

**Department:** Customer Service  
**Policy Owner:** Christian  
**Source Document Section:** Account Management & Membership

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes a clear and consistent procedure for escalating technical issues encountered by customers or staff within Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) systems, including the website, app, and membership platform. The aim is to ensure technical problems are addressed efficiently, minimising disruption to customers and maintaining a high standard of service.

---

## Scope

This procedure applies to all Customer Service staff handling customer enquiries related to technical issues, including but not limited to:

- Account access and login problems
- Website and app functionality errors
- Payment processing issues related to technical faults
- Issues with applying discount codes or accessing content
- Any other technical difficulties reported by customers

It covers all customer-facing channels (email, live chat, and website support forms) and internal action steps required for escalation.

---

## Procedure

### 1. Initial Troubleshooting

Customer Service staff must first attempt to resolve common technical issues by guiding the customer through standard troubleshooting steps, such as:

- Clearing browser cache and cookies
- Trying a different browser or device
- Force-closing and reopening the app
- Verifying correct login credentials
- Reinstalling the app (for app-related issues)

If these steps do not resolve the issue, or if the problem is clearly a technical fault beyond standard troubleshooting, proceed to escalation.

### 2. Gathering Required Information

Before escalating, staff must collect all relevant details to assist the development team in diagnosing and resolving the issue. This includes:

- A clear description of the problem
- The customer’s device type and model (for app issues)
- Operating system version (for app or device-specific issues)
- Screenshots or screen recordings illustrating the problem, if possible
- The customer’s email address and account details
- Any error messages received

### 3. Escalation to Development Team

- Escalate the technical issue to the development team as soon as it is identified as requiring technical intervention.
- Include all gathered information in the escalation request.
- Use the designated internal communication channel or ticketing system to submit the escalation.
- Clearly label the issue as “Technical Escalation” and provide a summary of the customer’s experience.

### 4. Customer Communication

- Inform the customer that their issue has been escalated to the technical team for further investigation.
- Advise the customer that they will be updated as soon as a resolution or further information is available.
- If possible, provide an estimated timeframe for follow-up or resolution.
- If there is an alternative way for the customer to access their content (e.g., using the website instead of the app), advise them accordingly.

### 5. Monitoring and Follow-Up

- Monitor the progress of the escalation and maintain communication with the development team.
- Provide updates to the customer as new information becomes available or when the issue is resolved.
- Once resolved, confirm with the customer that the issue has been fixed and offer any additional assistance if required.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Perform initial troubleshooting and information gathering.
  - Escalate unresolved technical issues promptly and with complete details.
  - Communicate clearly and empathetically with customers throughout the process.
  - Follow up with both the development team and the customer until resolution.

- **Development Team:**  
  - Investigate and resolve escalated technical issues.
  - Communicate progress and resolution status to Customer Service staff.

- **Policy Owner (Christian):**  
  - Ensure staff are trained on this procedure.
  - Review escalated cases for quality and consistency.
  - Update the procedure as needed based on feedback and operational changes.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- For customers based in China, support and escalation may be handled via the Wecom customer service channel instead of email.
- In urgent or high-impact cases, or if the customer is unable to access essential services (such as account login or payment processing), escalate the issue as a priority and notify the policy owner immediately.
- Do not attempt to resolve technical issues beyond the scope of Customer Service training; always escalate to the development team when required.

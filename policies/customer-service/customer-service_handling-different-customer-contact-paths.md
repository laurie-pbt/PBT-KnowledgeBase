# Handling Different Customer Contact Paths

# Handling Different Customer Contact Paths Policy

**Department:** Customer Service  
**Policy Owner:Ruby
**Origin:** "How to Handle Different Contact Paths" section

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes clear, consistent procedures for handling customer enquiries received through various contact paths. Its aim is to ensure that all customer interactions are managed efficiently, professionally, and through approved Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) channels. By doing so, the policy protects both the customer experience and staff wellbeing, reduces confusion, and maintains a high standard of service delivery.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all staff members involved in customer service, workshop support, marketing support, and shift-based support for PBT China. It covers all scenarios where customers may contact PBT, including direct messages, group chats, legacy channels, and social media.

---

## Policy

### 1. Official Customer Service Channel

- The main WeCom customer service gateway is the official and preferred route for all 1:1 customer enquiries in China.
- Staff must direct all customer support interactions to this channel wherever possible.
- The official WeCom channel link must be provided to customers when redirecting them from other contact paths.

### 2. Handling Legacy and Personal Contact Paths

#### 2.1 Old Personal WeChat Customer Service Phone

- If a customer contacts staff via the old personal WeChat customer service phone:
  - Staff must assist the customer politely and resolve the enquiry where possible.
  - After assisting, staff must inform the customer that future support should be requested via the official WeCom channel.
  - The tone must remain helpful and not abrupt.

  **Example Response:**  
  “Of course, I’m happy to help with this. For future support, please use our official PBT China customer service channel so the team can assist you more quickly and consistently.”

#### 2.2 Staff Personal WeCom

- If a customer contacts a staff member’s personal WeCom:
  - Staff must help the customer, especially if the matter is already in progress.
  - Staff must politely explain that future support should be through the official WeCom channel.
  - Staff must provide the official QR code or link.
  - Where appropriate, staff may also encourage the customer to join the community group or workshop interest group.

  **Example Response:**  
  “I’m happy to help you with this today. For future questions, please contact us through our official PBT China customer service channel so the team can support you quickly. I’ll send you the link here.”

#### 2.3 Staff Must Not

- Staff must not:
  - Promote their own personal QR code casually.
  - Encourage customers to message them directly at all hours.
  - Allow private contact habits to replace the official system.

### 3. Monitoring and Responding Across Customer-Facing Surfaces

- When on shift, staff are responsible for monitoring and responding to all relevant customer-facing surfaces, including:
  - Main WeCom customer service channel (primary)
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups
  - Social media groups, direct messages, and comments
  - Any other live customer chat groups or channels that require monitoring
  - Legacy surfaces (e.g., old customer service WeChat, personal WeCom contacts from historical staff relationships)
- Staff must prioritise the main WeCom channel, followed by groups, and only use personal accounts when absolutely necessary and then redirect back to official channels.

### 4. Group and Community Contact Paths

#### 4.1 Workshop Groups

- Customers should be encouraged to join the relevant workshop group via the group QR code when booking.
- If a customer is not yet in the workshop group, staff must:
  - Encourage them to join, or
  - Add them directly from the WeCom chat where appropriate.
- Workshop groups are used for practical updates, reminders, and support related to the workshop.

#### 4.2 Community Group and Workshop Interest Group

- Staff should encourage customers to join the PBT China Community Group and/or Workshop Interest Group when appropriate.
- This should be done naturally and helpfully, not forcefully.
- These groups are for ongoing engagement, support, and nurturing workshop interest.

### 5. Social Media and RedNote Monitoring

- Staff on shift must actively monitor assigned RedNote groups, social media groups, comments, and direct messages.
- The first priority is to monitor, spot issues, and reply where needed.
- The second priority is to nurture engagement and support soft conversion.

### 6. General Principles for All Contact Paths

- Staff must always use a friendly, calm, helpful, and professional tone.
- Staff must not argue with customers, guess answers, or use blunt or negative language.
- Staff must minimise personal ownership of customer relationships and ensure support is provided by PBT as an organisation.
- Staff must guide customers towards the correct next step, including bookings, group membership, or escalation where necessary.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Monitor and respond to all assigned customer contact paths during their shift.
  - Redirect enquiries from legacy or personal channels to the official WeCom channel.
  - Encourage appropriate group participation.
  - Maintain professional and helpful communication at all times.

- **Shift Leads/Active Representatives:**  
  - Ensure all relevant customer-facing surfaces are monitored during their shift.
  - Manage handovers and unresolved cases according to shift coverage rules.

- **Policy Owner (Christian):**  
  - Oversee the implementation and enforcement of this policy.
  - Review and update the policy as needed.

- **Escalation Owners:**  
  - Handle escalated cases according to the internal escalation paths.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- In exceptional operational circumstances, staff may use personal contact paths to resolve urgent customer issues but must always redirect future communication to the official channel.
- Staff should use judgement to complete conversations cleanly at the end of their shift, rather than handing over unnecessarily if the matter can be resolved quickly.

---

This policy ensures that all customer contact paths are managed in a way that is consistent, professional, and aligned with PBT’s standards of service.

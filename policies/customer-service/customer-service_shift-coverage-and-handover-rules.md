# Shift Coverage and Handover Rules

## Shift Coverage and Handover Rules Policy  
**Department:** Customer Service  
**Policy Owner:Ruby

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes clear, consistent rules for shift coverage and handover within the Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) China Customer Service team. Its aim is to ensure reliable, professional support for customers during all official service hours, minimise service gaps, and protect staff wellbeing by clarifying responsibilities for shift transitions and coverage.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all staff providing customer service, workshop support, and marketing support for PBT China, including those covering morning, evening, and Saturday shifts. It covers all scenarios where customer-facing support is provided, including 1:1 channels, group chats, and social media surfaces.

---

## Policy

### 1. Shift Coverage Requirements

- **Official Service Hours:**  
  - Monday to Friday: 9:30am – 10:00pm China time  
  - Saturday: 8:00am – 12:00pm China time  
  - During these hours, at least one staff member must be actively covering customer service.
- **Off-Hours:**  
  - Outside official hours, staff are not expected to provide active support unless specifically arranged.
  - The WeCom internal knowledge base bot may respond, but live staff should not be disturbed when off shift except in exceptional operational circumstances.

### 2. Customer Service Roster

- Staff must adhere to the published roster for shift assignments.
- If unable to cover a shift due to illness, leave, or other reasons, staff must:
  - Notify the team as early as possible.
  - Request coverage from the team.
  - Ensure the shift is picked up and not assume coverage will happen automatically.

### 3. Shift Setup and Coverage

- Staff on shift must have open and monitor:
  - Main WeCom customer service channel
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups/channels
  - Social media inboxes and comments requiring support
  - Alice (internal system)
  - Current workshop schedule
- Staff on shift are responsible for monitoring and responding across all relevant customer-facing surfaces, including 1:1 messages, groups, and social media.

### 4. Handover Rules

#### 4.1 Core Handover Rule

- WeCom routing must be managed manually via the channel assignment process.
- Do not rely solely on WeCom working hours settings to stop chat assignment.
- A staff member may still be assigned chats unless the next representative has been added and the current representative removes themselves.
- Staff must remove themselves from channel assignment only once the transition to the next representative is confirmed as safe.

#### 4.2 Standard End-of-Shift Process

- When ending a shift, the active representative must:
  - Ensure all live conversations are either resolved or appropriately handed over.
  - Remove themselves from the WeCom channel assignment only after confirming the next representative is active and aware.

#### 4.3 Practical Flexibility

- Use judgement at shift end:
  - If a conversation can be resolved neatly within the next 5–10 minutes, it is preferable to finish rather than hand over.
  - Aim for a clean finish to customer interactions wherever possible.

#### 4.4 Handover of Unresolved Cases

- Only unresolved cases require handover.
- For unresolved cases:
  - Directly message the next representative.
  - Clearly explain who the customer is, the issue, and provide relevant context.
  - Ensure the next representative has seen and acknowledged the handover.

#### 4.5 Busy Periods

- During unusually high customer volume:
  - Staff may request another team member to join the main WeCom channel temporarily.
  - Multiple staff may be active simultaneously.
  - All staff must maintain consistent and professional communication.

### 5. Leave, Sickness, and Unavailability

- If unable to cover an assigned shift, staff must:
  - Notify the team as early as possible.
  - Request and confirm coverage.
  - Not assume coverage will occur automatically.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Monitor all assigned customer-facing surfaces during their shift.
  - Follow handover and shift coverage procedures as outlined.
  - Ensure a smooth transition at shift end, including proper handover of unresolved cases.
  - Remove themselves from channel assignment only after confirming the next representative is active.
  - Arrange coverage proactively in the event of illness, leave, or unavailability.

- **Team Members (for coverage):**  
  - Respond promptly to coverage requests.
  - Accept and acknowledge handover information for unresolved cases.

- **Policy Owner (Christian):**  
  - Oversee adherence to this policy.
  - Review and update the policy as needed.
  - Address any issues or exceptions arising from shift coverage or handover processes.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- During periods when new customers are not being assigned, staff may continue to reply to customers whose consultations are already in progress.
- There is no requirement for a formal daily handover note or spreadsheet; only unresolved cases must be handed over directly.
- Staff should use judgement to avoid unnecessary handovers for cases that can be resolved quickly at shift end.

---

This policy ensures that PBT China customer service remains reliable, professional, and consistent, while also supporting staff wellbeing and operational clarity.

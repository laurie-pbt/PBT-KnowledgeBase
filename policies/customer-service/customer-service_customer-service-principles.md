# Customer Service Principles

# Customer Service Principles Policy

**Department:** Customer Service  
**Policy Owner:Ruby
**Source Document:** Customer Service Principles

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear, consistent principles and standards for customer service provided by Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) China. This policy ensures that all customer interactions are handled professionally, warmly, and efficiently, fostering trust, community, and positive outcomes for customers. It addresses the need for reliable service, clear communication, and appropriate escalation, while protecting staff boundaries and ensuring a unified approach to customer support, relationship building, and conversion.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all PBT China customer service staff, including those providing workshop support, marketing support, morning/evening shift coverage, and any staff interacting with customers through official PBT China channels. It covers all customer-facing interactions, including 1:1 support, group communications, social media, and community management.

---

## Policy

### 1. Customer Service Standards

- Staff must help customers quickly, warmly, and accurately.
- All interactions must be friendly, calm, helpful, professional, and solution-led.
- Staff must not argue, guess, or use blunt, annoyed, or dismissive language.
- Customers must always be left with a clear next step.
- The customer is always right in tone and handling; staff must de-escalate, remain calm, and never become defensive.
- Use positive, helpful language and avoid negative or absolute statements.
- Staff must not build customer dependence on personal accounts or relationships.
- Customer service should also support appropriate sales and community engagement, guiding customers towards workshops, memberships, merchandise, or next steps as relevant.

### 2. Official Service Channels

- The main WeCom customer service channel is the official and preferred route for all customer enquiries.
- Staff must encourage customers to use the main WeCom channel rather than personal accounts.
- Group channels (workshop groups, community groups, interest groups) are secondary and should be used as appropriate.
- Personal accounts should only be used when operationally necessary, with customers redirected to official channels for future support.

### 3. Customer-Facing Surfaces Monitoring

- On shift, staff must monitor and respond to:
  - Main WeCom customer service channel
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups
  - Social media groups, direct messages, and comments
  - Any other assigned customer-facing channels
- The order of priority is: direct customer enquiries, group/community support, then nurturing and engagement.

### 4. Shift Coverage and Handover

- Customer service hours are:
  - Monday to Friday: 9:30am – 10:00pm China time
  - Saturday: 8:00am – Midday China time
- At least one staff member must be actively covering customer service during these hours.
- Staff are not expected to provide support outside these hours unless specifically arranged.
- At the end of a shift, staff must:
  - Manually transfer WeCom channel assignment to the next rep.
  - Complete ongoing conversations if they can be resolved within 5–10 minutes for a clean finish.
  - Hand over only unresolved cases by directly messaging the next rep with clear context.
- If unable to cover a shift due to illness or leave, staff must notify the team early and ensure coverage is arranged.

### 5. Handling Different Contact Paths

- Main WeCom channel: All support should occur here by default.
- Legacy/Personal accounts: Assist customers politely, then redirect them to the main WeCom channel for future support.
- Staff must not promote personal QR codes or encourage direct personal contact as a primary support route.

### 6. Workshop and Community Groups

- Customers should be encouraged to join workshop groups upon booking.
- Workshop groups are used for practical updates, reminders, and support related to workshops.
- Community and interest groups are for ongoing engagement, support, and nurturing leads.
- Staff should encourage group participation naturally during relevant conversations.

### 7. Complaint Handling

- The goal is to calm the situation, understand the issue, and move towards resolution.
- Staff must:
  - Listen and acknowledge the customer’s concern.
  - Avoid blame or defensive responses.
  - Escalate complaints internally as needed according to escalation paths.
  - Return to the customer with a clear answer or next step.
- Possible resolutions may include explanation, replacement access, rescheduling, coupons, compensation, refunds, or escalation.

### 8. Refunds, Discounts, and Promotions

- If three or more people join the same workshop together, a 10% discount applies.
- Customers are generally not refunded if more people join after their booking; alternative value may be offered if appropriate.
- Staff must not invent new offers or exceptions. Any requests outside standard process must be escalated for approval.

### 9. Membership, Certificate, and Retest Support

- Certificates are electronic and normally sent within 48 hours of workshop completion.
- If a customer does not pass, they receive a retest notice with instructions.
- Staff must confirm timing, workshop completion, and correct email addresses before escalating certificate issues.
- Membership or access issues should be escalated if not quickly resolved.

### 10. Shift Setup Requirements

- While on shift, staff must have open:
  - Main WeCom customer service channel
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups/channels
  - Social media inboxes/comments requiring support
  - Alice (AI assistant)
  - Current workshop schedule
- This ensures visibility, quick response, and proper guidance for customers.

  - Provide service according to this policy during assigned shifts.
  - Monitor all required channels and groups.
  - Escalate issues as per internal escalation paths.
  - Ensure proper handover and shift coverage.
- **Head of Customer Service China (Ruby):**  
  - Oversee customer service operations and ensure policy adherence.
  - Act as escalation owner for workshop customer complaints.
- **Workshop Coordinator & Marketing Manager (Elaine):**

- **Online Training Manager (Yang):**  
  - Escalation owner for online training and merchandise customer complaints.
- **Head of Merchandise Global (Lachlan):**  
  - Escalation owner for merchandise and membership access issues.
- **Company General Manager (Laurie):**  
  - Final escalation owner for staff and company-wide customer complaints.
- **All Staff:**  
  - Represent PBT China professionally and uphold the standards set in this policy.

## Notes / Exceptions

- Staff are not expected to provide support outside official hours unless specifically arranged.
- Personal accounts may be used for support only when operationally necessary, with customers redirected to official channels for future contact.
- In busy periods, multiple staff may cover the main channel, but communication must remain consistent and professional.

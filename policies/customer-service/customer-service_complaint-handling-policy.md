## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to provide clear, consistent guidelines for handling customer complaints within Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) China. The policy ensures that all complaints are managed in a professional, calm, and solution-led manner, aiming to resolve issues efficiently while maintaining trust and a positive relationship with customers. This supports PBT’s commitment to high standards of customer service and organisational reputation.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all PBT China customer service staff, including those supporting workshops, marketing, and shift operations. It covers all customer complaints received through official channels, group chats, social media, and legacy contact routes. The policy is relevant to any scenario where a customer expresses dissatisfaction or raises a concern about PBT’s products, services, or staff conduct.

---

## Policy

### 1. Principles of Complaint Handling

- Staff must approach every complaint with the goal of calming the situation, understanding the issue, and moving towards a fair resolution.
- The objective is not to “win” the conversation, but to ensure the customer feels heard, respected, and supported.
- All communication must be friendly, calm, helpful, professional, and solution-led.
- Staff must never argue with customers, become defensive, or use blunt or negative wording.
- If unsure about a response, staff must not guess; instead, they should confirm information or escalate as appropriate.

### 2. Complaint Handling Procedure

#### Step 1: Acknowledge and Listen

- Respond promptly to the customer, acknowledging their concern.
- Use empathetic and positive language, such as:  
  “Thank you for letting us know, and I’m sorry for the frustration. Let me check this properly for you so we can give you the best next step.”

#### Step 2: Gather Information

- Clarify the details of the complaint to fully understand the issue.
- Avoid making assumptions or promises that cannot be fulfilled.

#### Step 3: Assess and Escalate

- Determine if the complaint can be resolved immediately or if it requires escalation.
- Escalate according to the following internal paths:
  - Workshop complaints: Elaine, then Kaisey if needed
  - Online training complaints: Yang, then Lachlan if needed
  - Merchandise complaints: Yang, then Lachlan if needed
  - Staff complaints: Laurie
  - Company-wide complaints: Laurie
- Customers do not need to know the names of escalation owners; communication should remain centralised through official channels.

#### Step 4: Provide a Solution or Next Step

- Return to the customer with a clear answer or the next step as soon as possible.
- Possible resolutions may include:
  - Explanation
  - Replacement access or assistance
  - Delay or reschedule
  - Coupon or compensation
  - Refund or exception (subject to management approval)
  - Internal escalation for further review

#### Step 5: Follow Up

- Ensure the customer is satisfied with the resolution or understands the next steps.
- Where appropriate, encourage continued engagement with PBT’s community or services.

### 3. Communication Standards

- Use warm, positive, and helpful language at all times.
- Avoid negative phrases such as “That’s not our fault” or “There’s nothing we can do.”
- Reframe responses to focus on what can be done, expected timelines, and who will assist.
- Do not promote personal contact details or encourage customers to bypass official channels.

### 4. Special Considerations

- For requests involving refunds, discounts, or exceptions, staff may only explain approved rules and must not invent new offers or promises.
- If a request falls outside standard procedures, staff must escalate for management judgement and approval before committing to any action.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Handle all complaints according to this policy.
  - Escalate unresolved or complex complaints to the appropriate internal owner.
  - Maintain professionalism and positive communication at all times.
- **Shift Leads/Managers:**  
  - Support staff in complaint escalation and resolution.
  - Ensure all staff are trained in these procedures.
- **Policy Owner (Christian):**  
  - Oversee policy implementation and updates.
  - Ensure compliance and address any breaches of the policy.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- In exceptional operational circumstances, staff may be contacted outside of standard hours, but this should be minimised and only when necessary.
- Staff must not assume that exceptions or refunds can be granted without explicit management approval.
- This policy applies to all customer-facing surfaces, including WeCom, group chats, social media, and legacy contact routes.

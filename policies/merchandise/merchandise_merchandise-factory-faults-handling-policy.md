## Purpose
To establish a consistent global approach for handling factory faults in Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) merchandise, ensuring compliance with consumer protection laws and providing a fair, transparent process for customers.

## Scope
This policy applies to all equipment and merchandise produced by PBT factories and suppliers, and governs all customer service and merchandising staff involved in fault assessment and resolution.

## Policy Statement
1. **Reporting Factory Faults**
   - Customers must provide clear evidence of the suspected factory fault, including photos or videos, a description of the fault, the item name, and their order number.
   - The order number must be the official order number generated by Shopify at the time of purchase.
   - Faults must be reported within the statutory warranty period applicable in the customer's country, generally up to twelve months from purchase, but subject to local consumer law.

2. **Assessment Process**
   - Customer Service Team will review all submitted evidence and assess whether the issue is likely a factory fault or normal wear and tear.
   - Contextual factors, such as the age of the product, will be considered (e.g. faults reported within six weeks are more likely to be factory faults than those reported after twelve months).

3. **Documentation**
   - All reported faults must be recorded in the designated fault spreadsheet, including the Shopify order number, photos, description of the fault, and the item involved.

4. **Remedy**
   - If a factory fault is confirmed, PBT will expedite a replacement product for the customer.
   - If the customer requests a refund or store credit instead of a replacement, the Customer Service Team will refer to the relevant consumer law in the customer’s country to determine the appropriate remedy.

5. **Dispute Resolution**
   - If the Customer Service Team cannot resolve whether a fault is due to manufacturing or wear and tear, the case must be escalated to the Head of Product.
   - The Head of Product will review all evidence and correspondence, consult with the factory if necessary, and provide a final decision based on the facts and relevant local laws.

## Exceptions
- Claims made outside the statutory warranty period for the customer’s country are not eligible for remedy unless required by local law.
- This policy does not apply to faults resulting from misuse, neglect, or unauthorised modifications.

## Escalation
- Unresolved or complex cases must be escalated to the Head of Product for final review and decision.
- The Head of Product’s decision is final, subject to compliance with relevant consumer protection laws.

## Review Schedule
This policy will be reviewed every six months to ensure ongoing compliance with global and local consumer laws and to address any emerging issues in factory fault handling.

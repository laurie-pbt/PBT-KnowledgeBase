# Workshop Payment Handling Procedure

# Workshop Payment Handling Procedure

**Department:** Workshops  
**Policy Owner:** Kaisey  
**Source:** Workshops

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes the procedures and requirements for handling payments related to Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) workshops. It aims to ensure secure, consistent, and customer-focused management of workshop payments, including accepted payment methods, alternative arrangements, payment plans, group discounts, and the issuing of receipts. The policy also addresses troubleshooting failed payments and outlines the process for manual payments where necessary.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to all staff involved in the administration, coordination, and customer service of PBT workshops, including area managers, workshop coordinators, and customer service representatives. It covers all in-person and online PBT and PCT workshops globally, including special procedures for specific regions as noted.

---

## Procedure

### 1. Standard Payment Methods

- All workshop bookings must be processed through the PBT website (www.pbt.dance).
- Accepted payment methods via the website include:
  - Debit card
  - Credit card
  - Alipay
- For workshops in South America, additional payment methods may be available during registration.

### 2. Payment Failures and Troubleshooting

- If a customer’s payment is rejected:
  - Advise the customer that, as PBT is an Australian company, their bank may flag the payment as an international transaction.
  - Instruct the customer to contact their bank to temporarily disable any block on international payments.
  - If the issue persists, offer alternative solutions as outlined below.

#### Alternative Payment Arrangements

- For customers in the UK, Europe, Canada, or USA:
  - If the workshop is at least 7 days away and below 90% sold, staff may offer to invoice the customer through Xero for a bank transfer as a last resort.
  - After sending the invoice, snooze the follow-up email for 2 days. If there is no response and the customer has not registered, follow up once.
- For customers in other regions:
  - Bank transfer is not offered except in exceptional circumstances. Customers must resolve the issue with their bank.

### 3. Bank Transfer Payments

- Bank transfer is a last resort and only permitted when:
  - The workshop is at least 7 days away.
  - The workshop is less than 90% full.
- Procedure:
  1. Create an invoice in Xero using the appropriate currency and branding for the customer’s country.
     - For non-Australian customers: Set ‘Amounts are’ to ‘No tax’.
     - For Australian customers: Use ‘Tax inclusive’ with GST on Income.
  2. Request the customer’s full name (for certificate), mobile number, and emergency contact details.
  3. Once remittance is received, manually register the customer for the workshop.
  4. Set a 3-day reminder to check that funds have arrived and mark the invoice as paid.

### 4. Payment Plans

- Some workshops may offer a payment plan option.
- If available, the payment plan will be displayed on the registration page during checkout.
- All final payments must be completed before the workshop date.
- If a payment plan is not shown at checkout, it is not available for that workshop.

### 5. Split Payments (Multiple Cards)

- The website does not support payment with multiple cards.
- If a customer requests to split payment:
  - Instruct them to contact customerservice@pbt.dance.
  - Arrange for payment via alternative means and manually register the customer once both payments are received.
  - Snooze the email for 3 days to check for payment; if unpaid after follow-up, cancel the booking and any outstanding invoices.

### 6. Group Discounts

- Group discounts are available only if the workshop is less than 90% sold out:
  - 3–5 people: 20% discount
  - 6–9 people: 30% discount
  - 10+ people: Forward the enquiry to the area manager and cc kaisey@pbt.dance
- To process a group discount:
  1. Create a personalised coupon code in workshop admin (Actions > Discount Coupons > Add Coupon).
  2. Set the maximum redemptions to the group size.
  3. Provide the code to the customer for use on the payment page during registration.

### 7. Coupon Codes

- Customers can enter a coupon code on the payment page after entering registration details by clicking “Add promotion code”.
- If a customer has trouble using a code, staff must assist via customerservice@pbt.dance.

### 8. Issuing Invoices and Receipts

- Customers can access invoices by logging into their PBT account and navigating to “Account” > “View / Track Invoices”.
- For Australian customers, invoice amounts include GST.
- For international customers, payments are processed as international transactions and do not include local taxes.
- If a customer does not have an account (e.g., workshop-only attendee), provide a free month so they can create an account and access their invoices.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Workshop Staff and Customer Service Representatives:**
  - Ensure all payments are processed according to this policy.
  - Provide clear instructions to customers regarding payment options and troubleshooting.
  - Process alternative payment arrangements (bank transfer, split payment) only under the specified conditions.
  - Issue invoices, receipts, and coupon codes as required.
  - Follow up on outstanding payments and cancel bookings if payment is not received within the specified timeframe.

- **Area Managers:**
  - Approve and process group discounts for groups of 10 or more.
  - Oversee manual registrations and ensure compliance with regional payment procedures.

- **Policy Owner (Kaisey):**
  - Maintain and update this policy as needed.
  - Provide guidance on exceptions and escalations.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- **Bank Transfer:** Only available as a last resort for eligible regions and workshops, as detailed above.
- **Extreme Circumstances:** In cases of health emergencies, accidents, or other exceptional situations, staff may escalate to the area manager or policy owner for approval of exceptions to standard payment procedures.
- **Regional Variations:** Payment methods and procedures may differ for workshops in South America and China. Always refer to the latest internal notes for region-specific instructions.
- **China-Specific:** For payments within China or from the Chinese company, contact Yang to arrange payment. For payments outside China by Chinese staff, send invoices to invoices@pbt.dance.

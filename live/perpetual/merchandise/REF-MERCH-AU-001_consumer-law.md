# Summary
Australian Consumer Law (ACL) overrides store policy where applicable.

# When it applies
The shipping or billing address is in Australia.

# Rules
- Goods must be of acceptable quality, fit for purpose, and as described.
- Remedies apply regardless of store warranty periods.
- Major fault: the customer chooses a refund or replacement.
- Minor fault: PBT may choose repair or replacement.
- Change of mind: ACL does not require refunds; the store policy may allow them at discretion.
- Overrides: base warranty limits do not restrict ACL rights.

# Allowed actions
- Provide a refund or replacement for major faults at the customer's choice.
- Choose repair or replacement for minor faults.
- Apply the change-of-mind policy when it is offered as a courtesy.

# Disallowed actions
- Deny ACL remedies due to store warranty periods or policy limits.
- State that change-of-mind refunds are required under ACL.

# Required wording
"Under Australian Consumer Law, you're entitled to a remedy if a product has a major fault."

# Escalation
Customer cites ACL, or the dispute involves high-value orders.

# Examples
- A product fails due to a major defect; the customer chooses a refund under ACL.
- A customer requests a change-of-mind return; clarify that ACL does not require it and apply store policy if eligible.

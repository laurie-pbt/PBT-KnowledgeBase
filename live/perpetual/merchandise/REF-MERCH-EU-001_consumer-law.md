# Summary
EU statutory consumer rights override store return policies where applicable.

# When it applies
The shipping or billing address is in the European Union.

# Rules
- Customers have a 14-day cooling-off period from receiving goods.
- Refunds include the product price and standard outbound shipping, and must be processed within 14 days of the return notice.
- Faulty goods carry a minimum 2-year guarantee.
- Overrides: store limits do not restrict statutory rights.

# Allowed actions
- Approve cooling-off returns within 14 days of delivery.
- Issue refunds that include standard outbound shipping when applicable.
- Provide remedies for faulty goods within the minimum 2-year guarantee.

# Disallowed actions
- Deny a valid cooling-off request made within 14 days.
- Exclude standard outbound shipping from a statutory refund.
- Reduce remedies below the 2-year guarantee for faulty goods.

# Required wording
"Under EU consumer law, you're entitled to a 14-day cooling-off period for online purchases."

# Escalation
Rights disputes or unclear eligibility.

# Examples
- A customer notifies support within 14 days of delivery; process the cooling-off return.
- A defect appears after 18 months; the statutory guarantee still applies.

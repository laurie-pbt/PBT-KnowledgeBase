# Community Group and Workshop Interest Group Policy

## Community Group and Workshop Interest Group Policy

**Department:** Customer Service  
**Policy Owner:Ruby
**Origin:** Community Group and Workshop Interest Group

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes the rules and procedures for managing and promoting the PBT China Community Group and PBT Workshop Interest Group. These groups are essential for fostering customer connection, providing ongoing support, nurturing workshop interest, and supporting the overall customer journey with Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT). The policy ensures that staff handle group invitations, participation, and communication in a consistent, professional, and customer-focused manner.

## Scope

This policy applies to all PBT China customer service staff, including those involved in workshop support, marketing support, and any staff covering morning or evening shifts. It covers all scenarios where staff interact with customers regarding community group and workshop interest group participation, including direct customer conversations, group management, and related customer service activities.

## Policy

### 1. Group Maintenance and Purpose

- PBT China must maintain two long-term groups:
  - **PBT China Community Group:** For general PBT connection, low-pressure support, fostering customer belonging, ongoing engagement, and providing a space for questions and encouragement.
  - **PBT Workshop Interest Group:** For workshop updates, launch notices, early reminders, nurturing interested leads, and making workshop conversion easier over time.

### 2. Customer Invitation and Promotion

- Staff must encourage customers to join one or both groups when it is naturally appropriate during customer conversations.
- Invitations should be offered in a helpful, non-intrusive manner, tailored to the customer's expressed interests or needs.
  - Example scenarios for invitation:
    - If a customer says they are not ready to book yet, want to know about future workshops, or are interested but undecided, staff should invite them to the workshop interest group for updates.
    - Customers seeking general connection or support should be invited to the community group.
- Example invitation language:
  - “You’re very welcome to join our workshop interest group so you can receive updates and future workshop information.”
  - “You can also join our community group if you’d like to stay connected and ask questions.”

### 3. Group Communication Standards

- All communication within the groups must be:
  - Friendly, calm, helpful, and professional.
  - Solution-led, aiming to leave customers feeling helped, respected, and clear on next steps.
- Staff must prioritise responding to direct questions, complaints, booking issues, or confusion before engaging in general nurturing or community-building conversation.
- Group messages should be practical, easy to act on, and reinforce important reminders or updates relevant to the group’s purpose.

### 4. Monitoring and Coverage

- When on shift, staff must have the following open and actively monitored:
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - Other relevant customer-facing surfaces (e.g., workshop groups, RedNote groups, social media channels)
- Staff are responsible for monitoring, spotting issues, and replying where needed in these groups during their assigned shifts.

### 5. Minimising Personal Ownership

- Staff must not encourage customers to rely on personal WeCom or private relationships for group invitations or support.
- The invitation and support process should remain centralised through official PBT channels and groups.

### 6. Handover and Shift Change

- During shift changes, staff must ensure that group monitoring responsibilities are clearly handed over if there are unresolved issues or ongoing conversations requiring follow-up.
- Only unresolved cases or active issues need to be handed over directly to the next staff member.

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Proactively invite and support customers in joining the appropriate groups.
  - Monitor and respond to group activity during assigned shifts.
  - Uphold communication standards and ensure a positive, helpful group environment.
  - Avoid building personal dependence and keep group management centralised.
  - Ensure proper handover of unresolved group issues at shift change.

- **Customer Service Shift Lead / Escalation Owners:**  
  - Oversee group management and support staff in handling complex or escalated group issues.
  - Ensure that group policies are followed and that coverage is maintained.

- **Policy Owner (Christian):**  
  - Maintain and update this policy as needed.
  - Ensure staff are trained and aware of their responsibilities regarding community and workshop interest groups.

## Notes / Exceptions

- Group invitations should always be context-sensitive and never feel forced or sales-driven.
- If a customer requests not to be added or wishes to leave a group, staff must respect this immediately and confirm their preferences.
- Any requests or issues falling outside standard group procedures must be escalated to the appropriate escalation owner for review and approval.

---

This policy ensures that the PBT China Community Group and PBT Workshop Interest Group are managed in a way that supports customer engagement, nurtures workshop interest, and maintains a professional, organised, and customer-focused environment.

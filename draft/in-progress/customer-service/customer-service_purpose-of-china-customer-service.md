# Purpose of China Customer Service

## Purpose of China Customer Service Policy

### Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish a clear, consistent, and professional approach for all Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) China-facing staff in delivering customer service, managing handovers, supporting groups, assisting with sales, and handling escalations. This policy ensures that every customer interaction is handled quickly, warmly, and accurately, guiding customers to the correct PBT workshop, membership, merchandise, or next step. It also aims to centralise customer communication within approved PBT channels, minimise personal ownership of customer relationships, and convert support conversations into trust, community, and sales where appropriate. The overarching goal is for every customer to feel helped, respected, and clear on what to do next, reflecting PBT’s values of friendliness, calmness, helpfulness, professionalism, and solution-led service.

### Scope

This policy applies to all staff involved in PBT China customer service, including but not limited to:
- Customer service representatives
- Workshop support staff
- China marketing support staff
- Morning and evening shift support staff

It covers all customer-facing interactions, group management, sales support, escalation procedures, and the use of official communication channels for PBT China.

### Policy

#### 1. Customer Service Standards

- Staff must provide service that is friendly, calm, helpful, professional, and solution-led.
- Staff must not argue with customers, guess answers, or use blunt, annoyed, or dismissive language.
- Every interaction should leave the customer with a clear next step and a positive impression of PBT.
- Staff must use positive, warm, and helpful language at all times.

#### 2. Official Communication Channels

- The main WeCom customer service gateway is the official and preferred route for all 1:1 customer enquiries.
- Customers should be encouraged to use official PBT channels rather than contacting individual staff members directly.
- Personal accounts should only be used when operationally necessary and customers must be redirected back to official channels for future support.
- Staff must not promote their personal QR codes or encourage direct, private contact outside official channels.

#### 3. Customer-Facing Surfaces

- Staff on shift are responsible for monitoring and responding across all relevant customer-facing surfaces, including:
  - Main WeCom customer service channel
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups
  - Social media groups, direct messages, and comments
  - Legacy and older contact routes still used by customers

#### 4. Service Hours and Shift Coverage

- Official customer service hours are:
  - Monday to Friday: 9:30am to 10:00pm China time
  - Saturday: 8:00am to Midday China time
- At least one staff member must be actively covering customer service during these hours.
- Outside these hours, the WeCom internal knowledge base bot may respond, but live staff are not expected to provide support unless specifically arranged.
- Staff must not be disturbed when off shift except in exceptional operational circumstances.

#### 5. Shift Handover Procedures

- At the end of each shift, staff must ensure WeCom routing is handled manually through channel assignment.
- Staff must not rely solely on WeCom working hours settings to stop chat assignment.
- Unresolved cases must be handed over directly to the next rep with clear context and confirmation that the handover has been received.
- If a case can be resolved within 5–10 minutes at the end of a shift, staff should finish the conversation rather than hand over.
- In busy periods, more than one staff member may be active in the main WeCom channel to ensure coverage.
- If a staff member is unavailable due to illness or leave, they must notify the team early and ensure their shift is covered.

#### 6. Group and Community Management

- Workshop groups are used for practical updates, reminders, and support relating to workshops.
- Customers should be encouraged to join workshop groups via QR codes upon booking.
- Staff must check if customers are in the correct group and add or invite them as needed.
- PBT China Community Group and PBT Workshop Interest Group are maintained for ongoing engagement, support, and nurturing leads.
- Staff should encourage customers to join these groups when appropriate, in a helpful and non-intrusive manner.

#### 7. Complaint Handling

- Staff must handle complaints by calming the situation, understanding the issue, and moving towards resolution.
- Staff must not attempt to "win" the conversation, become defensive, or use negative language.
- Possible resolutions include explanation, replacement access, rescheduling, coupons, compensation, refunds, or escalation as appropriate.
- Staff must escalate issues according to the internal escalation paths when required.

#### 8. Refunds, Discounts, and Promotions

- If three or more people join the same workshop together, they are eligible for a 10% discount.
- Customers who have already booked are generally not refunded due to later group bookings.
- Alternative forms of value may be offered instead of refunds, subject to management approval.
- Staff must not invent new offers or exceptions and must escalate requests outside the standard process.

#### 9. Membership, Certificate, and Retest Support

- Staff must be able to support customers with membership, certificates, and retests.
- Certificates are electronic and normally sent within 48 hours after workshop completion.
- Retests require written answers and video submissions.
- Staff must escalate unresolved membership or access issues to the designated internal owners.

#### 10. Monitoring and Shift Setup

- While on shift, staff must have open and monitor:
  - Main WeCom customer service
  - Workshop groups
  - PBT China Community Group
  - PBT Workshop Interest Group
  - RedNote groups/channels
  - Social media inboxes/comments
  - Alice (internal support tool)
  - Current workshop schedule
- This ensures visibility, quick response, and proper guidance for customers.

### Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff**: Deliver service according to this policy, monitor all assigned surfaces, follow handover and escalation procedures, and represent PBT China professionally.
- **Shift Leads/Coordinators**: Ensure roster coverage, manage handovers, and support staff in following procedures.
- **Escalation Owners**: Address escalated issues in their area of responsibility (e.g., workshop, online training, merchandise, certificates, membership, company-wide complaints).
- **Policy Owner (Christian)**: Maintain, review, and update this policy as required, ensuring it reflects current operational needs and standards.

### Notes / Exceptions

- Staff are not expected to provide live support outside official hours unless specifically arranged.
- Personal accounts may be used for support only when operationally necessary and must be redirected to official channels as soon as possible.
- In exceptional operational needs, off-shift staff may be contacted, but this should remain rare.

---

This policy ensures that PBT China customer service operates in a consistent, professional, and customer-focused manner, protecting both the customer experience and staff wellbeing.

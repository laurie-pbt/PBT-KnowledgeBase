# Policy on Pregnancy & Workshop Attendance

# Policy on Pregnancy & Workshop Attendance

**Department:** Workshops  
**Policy Owner:** Kaisey  
**Source:** Introduction

---

## Purpose

This policy provides clear guidance for teachers who are pregnant and wish to attend Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) Certification Teachers Workshops. It aims to ensure the safety, comfort, and well-being of pregnant attendees, while supporting their participation and learning experience. The policy also outlines the responsibilities of staff and tutors in accommodating pregnant attendees and sets expectations for communication and support.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All teachers attending PBT Certification Teachers Workshops (including all levels and PCT Teachers Certification Workshops), whether in-person or online.
- All PBT workshop tutors and customer service staff involved in workshop administration and attendee support.
- Scenarios where a teacher is pregnant at the time of booking or attending a workshop.

---

## Policy

### 1. Participation of Pregnant Attendees

- Pregnant teachers are welcome to attend any PBT Certification Teachers Workshop.
- It is common for teachers to attend workshops at various stages of pregnancy.
- Pregnant attendees are encouraged to participate only in exercises they feel comfortable with and to listen to their bodies at all times.

### 2. Exercise Modifications and Safety

- Certain PBT exercises are not recommended during pregnancy.
- Variations and modifications are available for some exercises to accommodate pregnant attendees.
- Pregnant attendees must inform the workshop tutor of their pregnancy upon arrival (or in advance, if possible).
- Tutors will provide advice, support, and alternative options throughout the course to ensure the attendee’s safety and comfort.
- The health and well-being of both the attendee and their baby is the highest priority.

### 3. Communication and Support

- Customer service staff must respond to pregnancy-related workshop inquiries with empathy and reassurance, confirming that attendance is welcome and support will be provided.
- Pregnant attendees should be advised to consult their healthcare provider regarding participation and to communicate any medical advice or restrictions to the tutor.

### 4. Booking and Workshop Logistics

- Workshop availability must be confirmed before booking. If a workshop is sold out, the attendee should be added to the wait-list.
- If the workshop is within 5 days, customer service must advise the attendee of the required equipment before booking, to ensure adequate preparation time.
- Pregnant attendees should be informed of any equipment requirements and provided with guidance on sourcing or bringing equipment as needed.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Pregnant Attendees:**  
  - Must communicate their pregnancy to the workshop tutor upon arrival or in advance.
  - Are responsible for participating only in exercises they are comfortable with and for following any medical advice from their healthcare provider.

- **Workshop Tutors:**  
  - Must provide appropriate exercise modifications and support to pregnant attendees.
  - Are responsible for ensuring the safety and comfort of all participants, with particular attention to the needs of pregnant attendees.

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Must respond to pregnancy-related inquiries with empathy, accurate information, and appropriate booking guidance.
  - Are responsible for notifying the area manager and tutor if a pregnant attendee is confirmed for a workshop, to ensure preparedness.

- **Area Manager:**  
  - Must ensure tutors are informed in advance when a pregnant attendee is expected, so that adequate support and modifications can be prepared.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- Pregnant attendees are not required to participate in all exercises and may choose to observe or rest as needed.
- If a pregnant attendee wishes to bring a baby to the workshop, this is generally discouraged. However, if all other options have been explored, PBT will do its best to accommodate, provided the baby’s presence does not distract other participants. In such cases, the area manager must be notified so the tutor can be informed in advance.
- The primary consideration at all times is the health and well-being of the pregnant attendee and their baby.

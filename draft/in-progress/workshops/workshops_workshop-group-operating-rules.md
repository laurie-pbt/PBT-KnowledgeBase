# Workshop Group Operating Rules

## Workshop Group Operating Rules

**Department:** Workshops  
**Policy Owner:** Kaisey  
**Origin:** Workshop Group Operating Rules

---

## Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to establish clear operating rules for the management and use of workshop groups within Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) China. Workshop groups are essential for delivering timely updates, reminders, and support to workshop participants, ensuring a consistent and organised customer experience. This policy aims to streamline group management, clarify staff responsibilities, and optimise communication with workshop attendees.

## Scope

This policy applies to all PBT China staff involved in workshop delivery, customer service, and support, including but not limited to the Workshops department, customer service representatives, and workshop coordinators. It covers all workshop groups created for PBT China workshops, regardless of delivery mode (in-person or online).

## Policy

### 1. Workshop Group Creation and Access

- Workshop groups must be created for each PBT China workshop to facilitate communication with participants.
- Upon booking, customers must be provided with the workshop group QR code and encouraged to join the group.
- Customer service representatives must check whether a customer who is booked into a workshop has already joined the relevant workshop group. If not, staff must:
  - Encourage the customer to join using the group QR code; or
  - Add the customer directly from the WeCom chat where appropriate.

### 2. Preferred Group Joining Flow

- The standard process is:
  1. Customer books a workshop.
  2. Customer receives the workshop group QR code.
  3. Customer joins the workshop group.
  4. Workshop updates and support are provided within the group.
- Staff must avoid manually inviting every customer one by one as the default process.

### 3. Workshop Group Purpose and Usage

Workshop groups are designated for the following functions:
- Sharing pre-course notices.
- Providing equipment and timetable reminders.
- Sending meeting and course-day updates.
- Issuing certificate notices and retest guidance where required.
- Offering post-course membership or next-step support.

### 4. Required Workshop Group Communications

Staff must use workshop groups to reinforce key reminders, including:
- Preparing all required equipment before the workshop.
- Checking email and course materials.
- Camera-on expectations for online training.
- Warnings regarding lateness, early leave, or incomplete participation as relevant.
- Certificate timing and delivery guidance.
- Post-course membership and retest process information.

**Example of a good workshop group message:**  
“Hello everyone, just a reminder to please prepare your PBT equipment before tomorrow’s workshop and check the course email carefully. Please join on time and keep your camera on throughout the class so the trainer can assess your work properly. We’re looking forward to seeing you.”

### 5. Official Communication

- All official communications to workshop customers within the group must be sent by the designated workshop coordinator (currently Elaine).

### 6. Integration with Other Groups

- Where appropriate, staff should encourage workshop participants to join the PBT China Community Group and/or the PBT Workshop Interest Group for ongoing support and updates.
- This encouragement should be natural and helpful, not forced.

## Responsibilities

- **Workshop Coordinator:** Responsible for creating workshop groups, distributing QR codes, sending official group communications, and ensuring all relevant updates and reminders are posted.
- **Customer Service Representatives:** Responsible for checking participant group membership status, encouraging or facilitating group joining, and supporting group communications as directed by the coordinator.
- **Workshops Department:** Accountable for maintaining the integrity and purpose of workshop groups in line with this policy.
- **Policy Owner (Kaisey):** Oversees policy implementation, reviews compliance, and updates the policy as needed.

## Notes / Exceptions

- In cases where a customer cannot join the group via QR code (e.g., technical issues), staff may add the customer directly from the WeCom chat if appropriate.
- Manual, one-by-one invitations should be minimised and only used when necessary due to customer circumstances.
- All group communications must adhere to PBT’s customer service principles: communications must be friendly, calm, helpful, professional, and solution-led.

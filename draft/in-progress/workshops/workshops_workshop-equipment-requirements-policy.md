# Workshop Equipment Requirements Policy

# Workshop Equipment Requirements Policy

**Department:** Workshops  
**Policy Owner:** Kaisey  
**Source Document:** Equipment Needed for the Workshop / Procedure for Customer Service

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes the requirements and procedures for equipment needed by participants attending Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) Certification Teachers Workshops. It ensures that all attendees are informed of the necessary equipment, are able to source it in a timely manner, and understand the options and limitations regarding equipment provision and borrowing. The policy aims to support a consistent, high-quality workshop experience and to address common customer service scenarios related to equipment.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All PBT Certification Teachers Workshops (including Level 1, Level 2, Level 3, and PCT Teachers Certification Workshops) held in any location or format (in-person or online).
- All workshop participants, customer service staff, tutors, and area managers involved in the organisation and delivery of PBT workshops.
- All scenarios where equipment requirements, sourcing, and borrowing are relevant, including late bookings and special circumstances.

---

## Policy

### 1. Standard Equipment Requirements

All participants must bring the following equipment to any PBT Certification Teachers Workshop:

- **Exercise Ball / Fit Ball**
- **3 x Fusion Balls:** Also known as small exercise balls or Pilates balls. Acceptable substitutes include toy balls. Required size: 20–22 cm (8–9 inches) in diameter.
- **Resistance Band (Theraband):** 3 metres long, medium strength, approximately 15 cm wide.
- **Massage Ball:** 6–8 cm in diameter.
- **Yoga Mat**

Participants are responsible for sourcing and bringing their own equipment unless otherwise specified.

### 2. Equipment Sourcing and Purchase

- For workshops scheduled more than 2 weeks in advance (except those in South America), participants should be directed to purchase equipment from the official online store at [www.pbtstore.com](https://www.pbtstore.com).
- For workshops in South America or those less than 2 weeks away, participants should be advised to source equipment locally, as delivery from the online store cannot be guaranteed in time.

### 3. Equipment Sale at Workshops

- If equipment will be available for sale at the workshop, participants do not need to be advised to source equipment in advance.
- Customer service must confirm whether equipment will be sold at a specific workshop before advising participants.

### 4. Borrowing Equipment

#### General Rule

- PBT does **not** provide extra equipment for borrowing at workshops, as tutors often travel from different regions and cannot transport spare items.

#### Exceptions and Procedures

- **Workshops More Than 2 Weeks Away (Areas A, B, C):** Participants are expected to source equipment themselves. They may bring balls deflated in a carry-on suitcase. Pumps will be available at the workshop for inflation.
- **Workshops Less Than 2 Weeks Away (Areas B, C, D):** Participants are still expected to source equipment, but if delivery is not possible, they should be advised to bring deflated equipment and use pumps provided at the venue.
- **Workshops Less Than 3 Days Away (Any Area, No Equipment for Sale):** If a participant is booked and unable to obtain equipment in time, customer service must:
  - Attempt to arrange for the tutor to lend spare equipment, if available.
  - If this is not possible, assist the participant in locating nearby stores where equipment may be purchased before the workshop.

### 5. Communication and Customer Service

- Customer service must always confirm the following before advising on equipment:
  - Workshop date and location
  - Whether equipment will be sold at the workshop
  - If the participant is already booked
  - The country/region of the workshop

- All communications regarding equipment requirements must include the full standard equipment list and clear guidance on sourcing or borrowing, as appropriate to the timing and location.

### 6. Special Considerations

- **Travel:** Participants may bring equipment deflated for ease of transport. Pumps will be provided at the venue.
- **Substitutions:** Toy balls of the correct size may be used in place of Fusion Balls.
- **Late Bookings:** For bookings within 5 days of the workshop, participants must be explicitly advised of the equipment requirements to ensure they have time to prepare.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Participants:** Responsible for sourcing and bringing all required equipment to the workshop.
- **Customer Service Staff:** Must provide accurate, timely information on equipment requirements, sourcing options, and borrowing procedures. Responsible for confirming workshop details and equipment sale status before advising participants.
- **Area Managers:** Support customer service in exceptional cases (e.g., last-minute borrowing requests) and coordinate with tutors regarding spare equipment availability.
- **Tutors:** May assist with lending equipment in exceptional circumstances, if possible, and should be informed by area managers of any special arrangements.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- **Workshops in South America:** Equipment cannot be guaranteed for delivery from the online store; participants must source locally.
- **Workshops with Equipment for Sale:** If equipment is sold at the workshop, participants do not need to source equipment in advance.
- **Emergency Borrowing:** In cases where participants are unable to obtain equipment within 3 days of the workshop and no equipment is for sale at the venue, customer service and area managers must make reasonable efforts to assist, but provision of equipment cannot be guaranteed.

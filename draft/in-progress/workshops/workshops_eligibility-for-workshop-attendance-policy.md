# Eligibility for Workshop Attendance Policy

# Eligibility for Workshop Attendance Policy

**Department:** Workshops  
**Policy Owner:** Kaisey  
**Source Document:** Procedure for Customer Service

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes clear eligibility criteria and participation guidelines for individuals wishing to attend Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) workshops. It aims to ensure that all workshop attendees meet the necessary requirements, understand the intended audience, and are aware of any restrictions or special circumstances that may affect their participation. The policy also addresses inclusivity, safety, and the integrity of the workshop experience.

---

## Scope

This policy applies to:
- All individuals seeking to attend any PBT Certification Teachers Workshop (Levels 1, 2, or 3) or PCT Teachers Certification Workshop.
- All customer service staff, workshop coordinators, tutors, and area managers involved in the registration, administration, and delivery of PBT workshops.
- All workshop formats, including in-person and online workshops.

---

## Policy

### 1. General Eligibility

- **Minimum Age:**  
  - The standard minimum age for workshop attendance is 17 years old.
  - Individuals under 17 may be considered if they are aspiring to teach PBT to students. Such cases require clarification of the applicant's intentions and may be subject to further review.

- **Professional Background:**  
  - PBT workshops are open to a broad range of professionals in the dance and movement industry, including:
    - Dance teachers of any style
    - Pilates, yoga, and gymnastics instructors
    - Physiotherapists
    - Dance professors
    - Individuals involved in dance medicine
  - Workshops are **not open to dance students** who are not studying to teach.

### 2. Workshop Levels and Progression

- **PBT Teachers Workshops:**  
  - Offered in three levels: Level 1, Level 2, and Level 3.
  - While it is possible to register for multiple levels at once, it is recommended to complete Level 1 and apply its principles for at least three months before advancing to Level 2 to ensure full comprehension of foundational material.

- **PCT Teachers Certification Workshop:**  
  - Offered as a single-level certification.

### 3. Physical Considerations

- **Pregnancy:**  
  - Pregnant participants are welcome to attend and are encouraged to participate in exercises they are comfortable with.
  - Certain exercises may not be recommended during pregnancy; participants should notify the tutor upon arrival so appropriate variations and support can be provided.

- **Injury:**  
  - Individuals with injuries may attend and are encouraged to participate as able or observe as needed.
  - Attendees should consult with their doctor regarding any limitations and communicate these to the tutor for appropriate accommodations.

### 4. Special Attendance Scenarios

- **Bringing a Baby:**  
  - Bringing a baby to a workshop is not ideal. If all other options have been explored, the attendee may bring a baby, provided the baby's presence does not distract others. The area manager must be informed in advance so the tutor can be notified.

- **Multiple Attendees Sharing a Device (Online Workshops):**  
  - Multiple participants may share a device for online workshops, but each must be individually registered.

- **Non-Ballet Teachers:**  
  - Workshops are not exclusive to ballet teachers and are open to the broader movement and dance educator community as outlined above.

- **Attending Workshops in Other Countries (Online):**  
  - Participants may attend online workshops based in other countries, provided they are comfortable with the language of instruction and time zone.

- **In-Person Workshops Attendance:**  
  - In-person workshops must be attended physically at the venue; remote or virtual attendance is not permitted unless the workshop is specifically advertised as online.

### 5. Workshop Capacity and Waitlist

- **Sold Out Workshops:**  
  - If a workshop is sold out, individuals may be added to a waitlist and will be notified if a position becomes available.

### 6. Equipment Requirements

- All attendees are responsible for bringing the required equipment as specified in the workshop information.
- Borrowing equipment is generally not possible except in last-minute situations, in which case staff will attempt to assist on a case-by-case basis.

---

## Responsibilities

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Verify eligibility of registrants according to this policy.
  - Provide accurate information regarding workshop requirements and restrictions.
  - Add ineligible applicants to the waitlist or refer special cases to the area manager as needed.

- **Area Managers:**  
  - Review and approve exceptions (e.g., underage applicants, bringing a baby).
  - Inform tutors of any special circumstances affecting workshop attendees.

- **Tutors:**  
  - Accommodate participants with special physical considerations (pregnancy, injury) as notified.
  - Ensure a supportive and inclusive workshop environment.

- **Policy Owner (Kaisey):**  
  - Oversee the implementation and periodic review of this policy.
  - Address escalated eligibility queries and approve exceptional cases.

---

## Notes / Exceptions

- **Underage Applicants:**  
  - Individuals under 17 may be considered only if they are training to become PBT teachers. Each case must be reviewed individually.

- **Bringing a Baby:**  
  - Strongly discouraged; permitted only after all alternatives are explored and with prior notification to the area manager and tutor.

- **Workshop Attendance by Non-Teaching Students:**  
  - Dance students who are not studying to teach are not eligible to attend workshops.

- **Online Workshop Device Sharing:**  
  - Permitted only if all participants are registered.

- **Physical Participation:**  
  - Participation in exercises is at the discretion of the attendee, particularly in cases of pregnancy or injury, but communication with the tutor is required for safety and support.

---

This policy is effective immediately and supersedes any previous guidance regarding eligibility for PBT workshop attendance. All staff must adhere to these guidelines to ensure a consistent and fair experience for all participants.

# Workshop Payment Methods Policy

**Department:** Finance  
**Policy Owner:Kaisey
**Source Document:** Procedure for Customer Service

---

## Purpose

This policy establishes the accepted payment methods and related procedures for Progressing Ballet Technique (PBT) Certification Teachers Workshops. It aims to ensure clarity, consistency, and fairness in how payments are processed, including alternative arrangements such as bank transfers, payment plans, and split payments. The policy also addresses exceptional scenarios to maintain operational efficiency and customer satisfaction.

## Scope

This policy applies to all staff involved in the administration, registration, and financial processing of PBT Certification Teachers Workshops worldwide. It covers all scenarios where customers wish to pay for workshops, including requests for alternative payment methods, payment plans, and split payments. This policy does not apply to the sale of equipment or other non-workshop products.

## Policy

### 1. Standard Payment Method

- **Default Method:** All workshop registrations must be paid through the PBT website using the standard online payment gateway (credit or debit card) unless an exception below applies.

### 2. Bank Transfer Payments

- **Eligibility:** Bank transfer payments are permitted only if ALL the following conditions are met:
  - The workshop is at least 7 days away.
  - The workshop is less than 90% full.
  - The tutor is NOT Adam or Bev.

- **Procedure:**
  1. Issue an invoice via the appropriate Zoho invoicing account for the region where the workshop is held. The invoice must include bank account details and ticketing fees.
  2. Instruct the customer to send remittance advice after payment and provide registration details (full name for certificate, mobile number, emergency contact name and phone number).
  3. Manually register the customer for the workshop upon receipt of remittance.
  4. Set a reminder to check that funds have cleared within 3 days of remittance receipt and mark the invoice as paid.

- **Restrictions:**  
  - Bank transfer is NOT permitted if the workshop is less than 7 days away, over 90% full, or led by Adam or Bev.
  - If the workshop is sold out, inform the customer and do not proceed with bank transfer.

### 3. Payment Plans (Instalments)

- **Eligibility:** Payment plans are permitted only if ALL the following conditions are met:
  - The workshop is at least 4 weeks away.
  - The tutor is NOT Adam or Bev.
  - The workshop is less than 90% full.

- **Procedure:**
  1. Offer the customer a payment plan where the total fee is divided by the number of weeks (or fortnights if over 8 weeks) until the workshop.
  2. Create and schedule PayPal invoices for each instalment, with the final payment due at least 5 days before the workshop.
  3. Register the customer after the first payment is received and upon confirmation of required registration details.
  4. Monitor payments and ensure all invoices are paid before the workshop date.

- **Restrictions:**  
  - Payment plans are NOT permitted if the workshop is less than 4 weeks away, over 90% full, or led by Adam or Bev.
  - Full payment is required at the time of booking if a payment plan is not available.

### 4. Split Payments (Multiple Cards)

- **Eligibility:** Customers may pay using multiple cards if workshop positions are available.

- **Procedure:**
  1. Collect registration details and confirm how the customer wishes to split the payment.
  2. Create separate PayPal invoices for each payment amount, due within 3 days.
  3. Register the customer upon confirmation of payment for all invoices.
  4. If payment is not received within 7 days, cancel the invoices and inform the customer.

### 5. Handling Payment Rejections

- **International Payments:** If a customer's payment is rejected, advise them to contact their bank to remove any international payment blocks.  
- **Australian Workshops:** If payment is rejected for an Australian workshop, proceed directly to invoicing via Zoho and manual registration.

- **Escalation:** If payment issues persist and the customer cannot pay online, offer to invoice them via Zoho and manually add them to the workshop.

### 6. General Restrictions and Notes

- **No Alternative Payments:** For workshops that do not meet the eligibility criteria for bank transfer, payment plan, or split payment, only website payments are accepted.
- **Capacity and Tutor Restrictions:** No exceptions are made to the above eligibility criteria based on workshop capacity or tutor assignment.
- **Manual Registration:** All alternative payment methods require manual registration of the customer after payment confirmation.
- **Sold Out Workshops:** No alternative payment methods are permitted if the workshop is sold out.

## Responsibilities

- **Finance Department:**  
  - Ensure invoices are issued accurately and promptly via the correct regional Zoho or PayPal accounts.
  - Monitor incoming payments, confirm receipt, and update payment status in the system.
  - Set reminders for payment follow-up and invoice reconciliation.

- **Customer Service Staff:**  
  - Assess customer requests for alternative payment methods against policy criteria.
  - Communicate payment options and instructions clearly to customers.
  - Collect and verify all required registration details.
  - Liaise with Finance to ensure timely registration upon payment.

- **Policy Owner (Laurie):**  
  - Oversee policy implementation and compliance.
  - Review and update the policy as required.

## Notes / Exceptions

- **Special Circumstances:**  
  - In cases of ongoing payment issues or exceptional hardship, escalate to the Finance Department or Policy Owner for review.
  - No exceptions to tutor or capacity restrictions unless explicitly approved by the Policy Owner.
